---

INTRODUCTION
-------------
This report presents comparative chassis dynamometer test results for five
popular sub-compact and compact SUVs available in the Indian market as of 2023.
All vehicles were tested in identical conditions using a Maha MSR 500 AWD chassis
dynamometer. Results represent wheel power (not crank power).

Note: Wheel power is typically 10-15% lower than manufacturer-claimed crank power
due to drivetrain losses.

---

TEST CONDITIONS
----------------
Ambient Temperature: 24°C ± 2°C
Barometric Pressure: 1013 mbar
Relative Humidity: 55% ± 5%
Dynamometer Type: Maha MSR 500 (2WD mode, front drive)
Fuel: Commercial grade 91 RON petrol (all petrol vehicles)
Fuel: Commercial grade BS6 diesel (all diesel vehicles)
Tyre Pressure: Manufacturer recommended cold inflation
Test Runs per Vehicle: 3 (average of best 2 runs reported)
Warm-up: 15 minutes at moderate load before test

OBSERVATIONS
-------------
1. The Tata Nexon's turbocharged 1.2L engine delivers the widest flat torque band
   among tested vehicles, maintaining near-peak torque from 2,500 to 4,000 RPM.
   
2. The Honda Elevate, despite being naturally aspirated, shows impressive top-end
   power. The CVT allows the engine to stay in its power band efficiently.

3. The Kia Seltos showed the highest drivetrain loss (14.3%), suggesting slightly
   higher internal friction or gearbox losses compared to competitors.

4. The Maruti Brezza has the lowest absolute power figure but offers the best
   power-to-weight ratio among manual transmission vehicles tested.

5. All vehicles showed dyno power within 12-15% of manufacturer claims,
   which is consistent with expected drivetrain losses.
