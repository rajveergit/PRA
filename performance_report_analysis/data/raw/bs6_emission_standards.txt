SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION TO BS6
--------------------------------
The Bharat Stage VI (BS6) emission norms are the sixth iteration of emission
standards adopted by India to control vehicular pollution. India leapfrogged
directly from BS4 to BS6 in 2020, skipping BS5, marking a significant
milestone in India's efforts to reduce air pollution.

BS6 is broadly equivalent to Euro 6 standards used in Europe, bringing India
in line with global best practices for vehicular emissions.

Key changes from BS4 to BS6:
- NOx (Petrol): Reduced by 25% (from 0.08 to 0.06 g/km)
- NOx (Diesel): Reduced by 68% (from 0.25 to 0.08 g/km)
- Particulate Matter (Diesel): Reduced by 82% (from 0.025 to 0.0045 g/km)
- Mandatory On-Board Diagnostics (OBD2)
- Mandatory Particulate Filter for diesel vehicles

SECTION 3: BS6 PHASE 2 - REAL DRIVING EMISSIONS (RDE)
-------------------------------------------------------
Effective from April 1, 2023, all new vehicles must comply with
Real Driving Emissions (RDE) testing in addition to laboratory tests.

3.1 What is RDE?
RDE measures actual emissions during on-road driving using a
Portable Emissions Measurement System (PEMS) device attached to the vehicle.
This eliminates the gap between lab-tested and real-world emissions.

3.2 RDE Test Requirements
Test Route Composition:
- Urban driving: 29-44% of trip distance
  - Speed: < 60 km/h
  - Stop time: 6-22% of urban time
- Rural driving: 23-43% of trip distance
  - Speed: 60-90 km/h
- Motorway driving: 23-43% of trip distance
  - Speed: > 90 km/h (min 90 km/h for > 5 mins)

Test Conditions:
- Altitude: 0-700m (base test), 700-1300m (extended test)
- Temperature: 0°C to 30°C (base), -7°C to 0°C (extended)
- Trip duration: 90-120 minutes
- Trip distance: 48 km minimum

Conformity Factors (CF):
- NOx: CF = 2.1 (Phase 2), target CF = 1.0 by 2025
- PN: CF = 1.5

3.3 RDE Compliance Statement
A vehicle complies with BS6 Phase 2 RDE if:
NOx_RDE ≤ BS6_NOx_limit × Conformity_Factor
Example: 0.08 g/km × 2.1 = 0.168 g/km maximum allowed on road

SECTION 4: REQUIRED EMISSION CONTROL TECHNOLOGIES
---------------------------------------------------

4.1 Petrol Vehicles
Three-Way Catalytic Converter (TWC):
- Converts CO, HC, and NOx simultaneously
- Operating temperature: 400°C - 800°C
- Light-off temperature: ~250°C
- Conversion efficiency at operating temp: >95%

Oxygen (Lambda) Sensors:
- Pre-catalyst (upstream): Controls fuel injection for stoichiometric combustion
- Post-catalyst (downstream): Monitors catalyst efficiency

Evaporative Emission Control (EVAP):
- Activated carbon canister captures fuel vapours
- Purge valve releases captured HC to intake during driving

Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV):
- Recirculates crankcase blow-by gases to intake

Gasoline Particulate Filter (GPF):
- Required for direct injection petrol engines
- Filters particulate matter from exhaust
- Regenerates automatically during driving

4.2 Diesel Vehicles
Diesel Oxidation Catalyst (DOC):
- Converts CO and HC from diesel combustion
- Operating temperature: 200°C - 550°C

Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF):
- Mandatory for BS6 compliance
- Filters >99% of soot particles
- Periodic regeneration required (automatic above 60 km/h)
- DPF Warning: If DPF light illuminates, drive at 60+ km/h for 30 mins

Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) with AdBlue:
- Required for NOx reduction in diesel
- Uses urea solution (AdBlue / DEF) to convert NOx to N2 and H2O
- AdBlue consumption: approx. 3-5% of diesel consumption
- AdBlue tank capacity: typically 10-20 litres
- AdBlue refill interval: every 15,000-20,000 km

Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR):
- Recirculates a portion of exhaust gas to reduce combustion temperature
- Reduces NOx formation at source

SECTION 5: ON-BOARD DIAGNOSTICS (OBD)
----------------------------------------
BS6 mandates OBD2 compliance for all vehicles.

5.1 OBD2 Requirements
- Malfunction Indicator Lamp (MIL / Check Engine Light): Mandatory
- Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs): Standardized across manufacturers
- Communication Protocol: CAN bus (ISO 15765-4)
- OBD port: 16-pin J1962 connector

5.2 Monitored Systems (BS6 OBD Requirements)
- Catalyst monitoring
- Oxygen sensor monitoring
- EGR monitoring
- Evaporative system monitoring
- Fuel system monitoring
- Misfire detection
- Secondary air system
- DPF monitoring (diesel)
- SCR/AdBlue monitoring (diesel)

5.3 Readiness Monitors
Before emissions testing, all readiness monitors must show "Ready":
- Catalyst Monitor
- O2 Sensor Monitor
- EVAP Monitor
- EGR Monitor
- Fuel System Monitor
- Misfire Monitor

SECTION 6: FUEL REQUIREMENTS FOR BS6
---------------------------------------
Petrol: Minimum 91 RON (Research Octane Number)
Diesel: Maximum sulphur content: 10 ppm (parts per million)
         (BS4 diesel: 50 ppm | BS6 diesel: 10 ppm)

Note: Using BS4 fuel in a BS6 vehicle will damage emission systems.
AdBlue must meet ISO 22241 purity standards.

SECTION 7: IMPACT ON VEHICLE TECHNOLOGY & PERFORMANCE
-------------------------------------------------------
7.1 Effect on Petrol Engines
- Direct injection petrol engines require GPF
- Turbocharging more common to maintain performance while downsizing
- Fuel injection timing refined for lower emissions
- EGR used in some petrol engines

7.2 Effect on Diesel Engines
- DPF adds back pressure, slightly reduces power
- SCR system adds cost and complexity
- AdBlue system requires regular monitoring
- Diesel engines became significantly more expensive (+15-20%)

7.3 Industry Response
Due to the high compliance cost of BS6 diesel, many manufacturers have:
- Discontinued smaller diesel engines (below 1.5L)
- Reduced diesel variant offerings
- Focused on CNG/petrol/hybrid alternatives

Example: Maruti Suzuki discontinued all diesel models from its lineup in 2020
citing BS6 compliance costs for smaller engines.

SECTION 8: EMISSION TEST PROCEDURE (WLTP)
-------------------------------------------
BS6 uses the Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicles Test Procedure (WLTP)
replacing the older NEDC (New European Driving Cycle).

WLTP Test Cycles:
Phase          | Speed Range | Duration | Distance
---------------|-------------|----------|----------
Low            | 0-56 km/h   | 589 sec  | 3.095 km
Medium         | 0-76 km/h   | 433 sec  | 4.756 km
High           | 0-97 km/h   | 455 sec  | 7.158 km
Extra-High     | 0-131 km/h  | 323 sec  | 8.254 km
Total          | -           | 1800 sec | 23.27 km

WLTP fuel consumption figures are typically 10-15% higher than older
NEDC figures, providing more realistic real-world estimates.

---

SECTION 9: PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
-----------------------------------------
Under the Motor Vehicles Act and CMVR:
- Manufacturing/selling non-compliant vehicles: Up to ₹10,000 per vehicle
- Tampering with emission control systems: Up to ₹10,000 fine
- Operating vehicle with tampered system: PUC test failure + fine
